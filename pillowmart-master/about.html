About

Our Crafting
Journey

We are Crafters, and our story is one woven with passion, purpose, and the spirit of the earth. It began not in a grand boardroom, but within the vibrant energy of a college project at NSB Academy. What started as a simple idea – exploring the beauty of handmade craft and handcrafted artifacts – quickly grew into a profound commitment to sustainable creation.

Our journey is symbolised by the Earth Ox. More than just strength, this emblem represents our deep roots in nature, the honest work of traditional artisans and art crafters, and an enduring connection to the land that provides our materials. Like the ox patiently working the soil, we build our brand with care, integrity, and respect for the environment.

At Crafters, we believe every handmade piece tells a story. A story of skilled hands shaping natural materials, a story of sustainable practices that protect our planet, and a story of conscious choices that you make by bringing these handcrafted artifacts into your life. We meticulously source our natural materials, ensuring they are responsibly harvested and processed with minimal impact. We collaborate directly with artisans, celebrating their heritage techniques and ensuring fair livelihoods. Each item is truly handmade with love.

Unlike many products in the market, our handmade goods are born from a place of deep respect for the earth. They are crafted to be cherished, not discarded, embodying durability and timeless beauty.

Our Mission

To provide beautifully handcrafted, sustainably sourced artifacts that connect people to the earth, celebrate traditional skills, and inspire conscious living.

Our Vision

To be a leading voice and provider in the sustainable craft movement, fostering a global community that values authenticity, environmental responsibility, and the enduring beauty of handmade goods.

Our Goal

To continuously expand our network of artisans, innovate with new sustainable materials and designs, and make eco-friendly handcrafted artifacts accessible to a wider audience, promoting a circular economy where craft and planet thrive together.
